# UGC AD BREAKDOWN REFERENCE
Three canonical formats for DTC + creative agency UGC video ads.
Each breakdown uses the same four-section structure: shot-by-shot timeline (with dialogue), master effects inventory, density map, and energy arc.

================================================================
## FORMAT 1: PROBLEM-SOLUTION UGC
Reference: Skincare brand problem-solution ad — 22s, TikTok native
================================================================

### SHOT-BY-SHOT TIMELINE

SHOT 1 (00:00–00:02) — The Hook Beat
• EFFECT: Selfie-angle handheld + direct eye contact + pattern-interrupt line
• Creator holds phone ~20 inches from face, slight downward tilt, off-axis framing (slightly left of center)
• Natural micro-wobble throughout — handheld, not stabilized
• Lived-in background (bathroom counter visible, a candle, unbranded products in soft focus)
• Window light from camera left, no color grade
• DIALOGUE: "Okay I need to stop gaslighting myself into thinking my skin barrier is fine."
• Delivery: mid-energy, talking to a friend, slight exhale-laugh on "gaslighting"
• Exits on a natural head tilt that sets up the jump cut

SHOT 2 (00:02–00:05) — The Pain Reveal
• EFFECT: Jump cut (tightens pacing) + natural eye break
• Creator leans slightly closer to camera, eyes dart down and back up on "every single morning"
• Same framing, same lighting — feels like one continuous take with the filler cut out
• DIALOGUE: "Every single morning I wake up, my cheeks are red, my nose is flaky, and I look like I got in a fight with a washcloth."
• Delivery: self-deprecating, real — not a performance

SHOT 3 (00:05–00:07) — Product Insert B-roll
• EFFECT: Close-hold product reveal + focus hunt
• Cut to product held in-frame at an angle, hand visible at right edge
• Brief lens hunt as the product enters (focus catches up, adds authenticity)
• Product is foregrounded against the same bathroom environment from the A-roll
• NO DIALOGUE — a beat of ambient room sound only (creator's inhale is audible)

SHOT 4 (00:07–00:11) — The Discovery Beat
• EFFECT: Return to selfie-angle + off-the-cuff pivot + mid-sentence emphasis beat
• Back to creator's face, same framing as SHOT 1 but subtly tighter (she's moved closer to camera)
• Slight head tilt on "this"; eyebrow raise on "actually worked"
• DIALOGUE: "So I tried this, because a friend told me to, and after like five days it actually worked."
• Delivery: climbing energy, the line "actually worked" lands with slight surprise

SHOT 5 (00:11–00:14) — The Demo Insert
• EFFECT: Over-shoulder demo angle + product insert B-roll
• Cut to low-angle over-shoulder: camera looks past creator's jawline at her hand applying the product to her cheek
• Product visible in hand, application visible on face
• Natural motion, unhurried
• DIALOGUE (voiceover continues from SHOT 4): "It's this peptide cream that doesn't feel like anything but it literally fixed the redness."

SHOT 6 (00:14–00:17) — The Proof Beat
• EFFECT: Before/after split (same framing, two moments) + on-screen text overlay
• Frame splits vertically: left side is a still from earlier (red, flaky), right side is current (clear, calm)
• On-screen text appears in iMessage-style captions: "day 1 vs day 7"
• NO DIALOGUE — visual proof speaks, ambient room sound only
• This is the SIGNATURE MOMENT of the ad

SHOT 7 (00:17–00:20) — The Reaction Beat
• EFFECT: Reaction micro-expression + direct eye contact
• Back to creator's face, she half-laughs, slight shake of the head
• DIALOGUE: "I'm not exaggerating. I genuinely look like a different person."
• Delivery: low-key disbelief, not hype

SHOT 8 (00:20–00:22) — The CTA Close
• EFFECT: Direct eye contact + on-screen text overlay + CTA beat
• Creator holds product up next to her face, looks straight down the lens
• On-screen text: "link in bio — use code FRIEND15"
• DIALOGUE: "Okay that's it. Go try it."
• Delivery: warm, casual, not pushy
• Exits on a soft smile, natural trail-off

### MASTER EFFECTS INVENTORY

1. SELFIE-ANGLE HANDHELD (used 4x) — Shots 1, 2, 4, 7, 8. The primary framing. Defines the ad as UGC, not branded content.
2. DIRECT EYE CONTACT (used 3x) — Shots 1, 7, 8. Establishes the parasocial "she's talking to me" effect.
3. JUMP CUT ON DIALOGUE BEAT (used 2x) — Shots 1→2, 4→5. Tightens pacing without feeling edited.
4. PRODUCT INSERT B-ROLL (used 2x) — Shots 3, 5. Breaks up talking-head monotony, shows the product in context.
5. NATURAL EYE BREAK (used 1x) — Shot 2. The real-person tell — separates this from a scripted spot.
6. ON-SCREEN TEXT OVERLAY (used 2x) — Shots 6, 8. iMessage-style captions, not lower-thirds.
7. BEFORE/AFTER SPLIT (used 1x) — Shot 6. The proof shot. Signature visual.
8. REACTION MICRO-EXPRESSION (used 1x) — Shot 7. The authenticity anchor — a performed reaction would break the illusion.
9. OFF-THE-CUFF PIVOT (used 1x) — Shot 4. "Actually worked" — the tonal pivot from complaint to discovery.
10. FOCUS HUNT (used 1x) — Shot 3. Imperfection that signals "real footage."

### EFFECTS DENSITY MAP

00:00–00:05 = MEDIUM DENSITY (selfie-angle, handheld, direct eye contact, jump cut, natural eye break — 5 effects across 2 shots)
00:05–00:11 = LOW DENSITY (product insert, return to face, one micro-expression — slower beat, building the story)
00:11–00:17 = HIGH DENSITY (demo angle, before/after split, on-screen text, ambient-only audio — 4 effects in 6s, the proof block)
00:17–00:22 = MEDIUM DENSITY (return to face, reaction, CTA, text overlay — 4 effects but spread across the close)

### ENERGY ARC (three-act, problem-solution structure)

Act 1 (0–5s): THE PROBLEM — hook lands, creator identifies with viewer's pain, establishes authenticity.
Act 2 (5–17s): THE DISCOVERY + PROOF — product enters, demo shows use, before/after delivers the visual evidence. This is the heaviest density block — it earns the belief.
Act 3 (17–22s): THE RESOLUTION + CTA — reaction beat cements the outcome, CTA is warm not pushy. Energy resolves into a smile.

The structure is: *I have a problem → this product changed it → here's the proof → here's the ask.* The emotional journey moves from relatability (0–5s) to curiosity (5–11s) to belief (11–17s) to action (17–22s).

## FORMAT 2: DEMO-FIRST UGC
Reference: Kitchen gadget demo-first ad — 24s, TikTok native
================================================================

### SHOT-BY-SHOT TIMELINE

SHOT 1 (00:00–00:03) — The Demo Hook
• EFFECT: Over-shoulder demo angle + close-hold product reveal + hook dialogue
• Camera low, angled up past creator's shoulder at the kitchen counter
• Product is mid-use (e.g. gadget actively doing the thing) within the first half-second
• Creator's hand and forearm visible — the thing is HAPPENING on screen
• Window light, messy counter (a cutting board, a stray onion skin, a coffee mug — lived-in)
• DIALOGUE: "Wait — have you seen this thing?"
• Delivery: mid-surprise, like she just discovered it five minutes before filming

SHOT 2 (00:03–00:06) — The Context Reveal
• EFFECT: Cut to selfie-angle + direct eye contact + natural micro-wobble
• Creator turns phone toward her face, framing shifts from demo to face
• DIALOGUE: "I've been using this every single day for two weeks and I genuinely forgot what life was like before it."
• Delivery: playful hyperbole, smile at the end

SHOT 3 (00:06–00:09) — The Re-Demo (tighter)
• EFFECT: Close-hold product reveal + focus hunt
• Cut back to product, even tighter framing — you can see the mechanism working
• Brief focus hunt as camera adjusts
• DIALOGUE (voiceover): "Okay so the deal is—"
• Natural trail — sentence continues in next shot

SHOT 4 (00:09–00:13) — The How-It-Works Beat
• EFFECT: Over-shoulder demo angle + jump cut on dialogue beat
• Back to over-shoulder framing, creator is demonstrating a specific feature
• Jump cut mid-explanation to tighten pacing
• DIALOGUE: "—you just press this, and it does the thing in like ten seconds. Ten seconds."
• Delivery: leans on the repetition of "ten seconds" with a slight incredulous headshake

SHOT 5 (00:13–00:16) — The Pain Contrast
• EFFECT: Selfie-angle + off-the-cuff pivot + natural eye break
• Creator back to face, slightly lower energy for the setup line
• Eyes dart down briefly on "used to"
• DIALOGUE: "I used to spend like twenty minutes doing this. Genuinely. Twenty. Minutes."
• Delivery: flat disbelief, not performative frustration

SHOT 6 (00:16–00:19) — The Result Reveal
• EFFECT: Close-hold product reveal + on-screen text overlay
• Cut to the RESULT (e.g. finished output) held close to camera
• On-screen text appears: "10 seconds. not even joking."
• NO DIALOGUE — ambient kitchen sound, creator's inhale audible
• This is the SIGNATURE MOMENT

SHOT 7 (00:19–00:22) — The Endorsement
• EFFECT: Selfie-angle + mid-sentence emphasis beat + direct eye contact
• Creator back to face, slight head tilt on "single person"
• DIALOGUE: "I'm telling every single person I know about this. This is the thing."
• Delivery: certainty without hype, friend-recommending-a-friend energy

SHOT 8 (00:22–00:24) — The CTA Close
• EFFECT: Direct eye contact + on-screen text overlay
• Product visible in hand next to face
• On-screen text: "linked below 👇"
• DIALOGUE: "Seriously, go get one."
• Exits on a shrug, casual

### MASTER EFFECTS INVENTORY

1. OVER-SHOULDER DEMO ANGLE (used 3x) — Shots 1, 4, implied setup for 6. Defines this format — the product is the hero, not the face.
2. CLOSE-HOLD PRODUCT REVEAL (used 3x) — Shots 1, 3, 6. Three escalating product moments: intro, mechanism, result.
3. SELFIE-ANGLE (used 4x) — Shots 2, 5, 7, 8. The reaction/commentary frames that break up the demo beats.
4. DIRECT EYE CONTACT (used 3x) — Shots 2, 7, 8. The trust-building frames.
5. JUMP CUT ON DIALOGUE BEAT (used 2x) — Shots 3→4, 4→5. Pacing tightener.
6. FOCUS HUNT (used 1x) — Shot 3. Authenticity signal.
7. ON-SCREEN TEXT OVERLAY (used 2x) — Shots 6, 8. Proof caption + CTA caption.
8. OFF-THE-CUFF PIVOT (used 1x) — Shot 5. "I used to..." — the contrast pivot.
9. NATURAL EYE BREAK (used 1x) — Shot 5. Authenticity tell.
10. MID-SENTENCE EMPHASIS BEAT (used 1x) — Shot 7. Slight head tilt on "single person."

### EFFECTS DENSITY MAP

00:00–00:06 = MEDIUM DENSITY (demo angle, product reveal, selfie pivot, eye contact — 4 effects in 6s, the hook block)
00:06–00:13 = MEDIUM DENSITY (product re-reveal, focus hunt, jump cut, back to demo — 4 effects in 7s, the explanation)
00:13–00:19 = HIGH DENSITY (selfie pivot, eye break, close-hold result, on-screen text — 4 effects in 6s, the payoff block)
00:19–00:24 = LOW DENSITY (selfie, direct eye contact, CTA — the resolution)

### ENERGY ARC (demo-first structure)

Act 1 (0–6s): THE "WHAT IS THAT" MOMENT — product is on screen in the first frame. Hook is the demo itself, not a line. Face enters in the second shot to contextualize.
Act 2 (6–19s): THE "HERE'S THE DEAL" BLOCK — alternates between demo shots (showing) and face shots (telling). The pain-contrast in Shot 5 is the emotional pivot that makes the result in Shot 6 land.
Act 3 (19–24s): THE ENDORSEMENT + CTA — back to face, low-pressure ask. The energy lands on confidence, not urgency.

The demo-first format works because the product is already visually compelling. The creator's job isn't to sell it — it's to contextualize what the viewer is already watching.

## FORMAT 3: TESTIMONIAL UGC
Reference: Supplement testimonial ad — 26s, TikTok native
================================================================

### SHOT-BY-SHOT TIMELINE

SHOT 1 (00:00–00:03) — The Skeptic Hook
• EFFECT: Selfie-angle handheld + direct eye contact + pattern-interrupt statement
• Creator sitting (couch, car, kitchen — not standing, not posed), phone at face height
• Slight off-axis framing, natural micro-wobble
• Messy background — a throw pillow, a blurry TV in the distance, laundry visible
• DIALOGUE: "I did not think this was going to work. I'm going to be honest."
• Delivery: flat, slightly amused at herself, no enthusiasm yet

SHOT 2 (00:03–00:07) — The Setup Context
• EFFECT: Natural eye break + conversational pacing
• Same framing, creator leans back slightly, eyes drift up-and-left as she remembers
• DIALOGUE: "I bought it like four months ago because I saw it on here obviously, and I was like, fine. I'll be the guinea pig."
• Delivery: storytelling tone, the "obviously" is self-aware

SHOT 3 (00:07–00:10) — The Skepticism Beat
• EFFECT: Mid-sentence emphasis beat + natural eye break
• Small head tilt on "nothing"; eyes dart briefly
• DIALOGUE: "And the first two weeks, nothing. I was literally about to return it."
• Delivery: hands-up shrug mid-sentence, body language does the work

SHOT 4 (00:10–00:13) — The Turn
• EFFECT: Off-the-cuff pivot + direct eye contact + energy shift
• Creator leans forward slightly toward the camera
• DIALOGUE: "And then — okay this is the part that's annoying — it just started working."
• Delivery: slight laugh on "annoying," energy lifts here, first smile

SHOT 5 (00:13–00:17) — The Outcome Detail
• EFFECT: Close-hold product reveal + product insert B-roll
• Cut to product held at angle, hand visible, same lived-in background
• Product is foregrounded but not hero-shot — feels like she picked it up mid-conversation
• DIALOGUE (voiceover): "My energy's up, I'm not crashing at 3pm anymore, and my sleep is genuinely better."
• Delivery: list three specific things, not vague claims

SHOT 6 (00:17–00:20) — The Endorsement Pivot
• EFFECT: Return to selfie-angle + mid-sentence emphasis beat + reaction micro-expression
• Back to face, creator does a slight head-shake on "I don't care"
• DIALOGUE: "I don't care if it's placebo. It's working. That's my whole thing."
• Delivery: playful defiance, the honest-caveat is the trust anchor

SHOT 7 (00:20–00:23) — The Recommendation
• EFFECT: Direct eye contact + conversational pacing
• DIALOGUE: "If you've been staring at this for a month trying to decide — I was you. Just try it."
• Delivery: warm, genuine, addresses the lurker directly
• This is the SIGNATURE MOMENT — the testimonial's emotional peak

SHOT 8 (00:23–00:26) — The CTA Close
• EFFECT: On-screen text overlay + direct eye contact + soft exit
• Product visible in hand near face
• On-screen text: "my code is [NAME]15" or similar
• DIALOGUE: "Code's down there. That's it, that's the video."
• Exits on a wave or a small smile, natural trail-off

### MASTER EFFECTS INVENTORY

1. SELFIE-ANGLE HANDHELD (used 6x) — Shots 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8. The testimonial lives in the face — this is the most selfie-angle-heavy format.
2. DIRECT EYE CONTACT (used 4x) — Shots 1, 4, 7, 8. The trust currency.
3. NATURAL EYE BREAK (used 2x) — Shots 2, 3. The "I'm remembering this" tell.
4. MID-SENTENCE EMPHASIS BEAT (used 2x) — Shots 3, 6. The body-language-driven honesty moments.
5. OFF-THE-CUFF PIVOT (used 1x) — Shot 4. "And then—" — the tonal turn from skepticism to result.
6. CONVERSATIONAL PACING (used 2x) — Shots 2, 7. Natural pauses, not performative silence.
7. CLOSE-HOLD PRODUCT REVEAL (used 1x) — Shot 5. The one product moment — testimonial formats use less product footage than demo-first.
8. PRODUCT INSERT B-ROLL (used 1x) — Shot 5. The only B-roll in the whole ad.
9. REACTION MICRO-EXPRESSION (used 1x) — Shot 6. The "I don't care if it's placebo" smile.
10. ON-SCREEN TEXT OVERLAY (used 1x) — Shot 8. CTA caption only — no text during the story.

### EFFECTS DENSITY MAP

00:00–00:10 = LOW DENSITY (selfie, handheld, eye contact, eye breaks — 4 effects but all ambient, building the story)
00:10–00:17 = MEDIUM DENSITY (pivot, product insert, voiceover carry — the turn + detail)
00:17–00:23 = LOW DENSITY (return to face, emphasis, direct eye contact — the emotional peak is low-effect on purpose)
00:23–00:26 = LOW DENSITY (CTA text, eye contact, exit — the close lands soft)

The testimonial format is intentionally LOW DENSITY throughout. More effects would undermine the authenticity. This is the opposite energy of a problem-solution or demo-first ad.

### ENERGY ARC (testimonial structure — low and slow wins)

Act 1 (0–10s): THE SKEPTICISM — creator establishes she's not a shill. This is the credibility foundation. Every word is earning trust.
Act 2 (10–20s): THE TURN + DETAIL — "and then it just started working" is the tonal pivot. The three specifics (energy, 3pm, sleep) are the evidence block. The "I don't care if it's placebo" is the honest-caveat that cements belief.
Act 3 (20–26s): THE RECOMMENDATION + CTA — direct address to the lurker watching ("I was you") is the emotional peak. The CTA is almost throwaway — the work was already done.

Testimonials that try to be exciting fail. The power is in the flatness of the delivery, the specificity of the detail, and the honest-caveat that proves the creator isn't lying.

## UNIVERSAL UGC PRINCIPLES

Across all three formats, these principles always hold:

1. **The hook happens in the first 1.5 seconds or it doesn't happen.** Pattern-interrupt line, visual surprise, or product-in-motion — pick one.
2. **The face earns trust. The product earns belief. Alternate them.** Every UGC ad is a rhythm of face-shots and product-shots. Too much face = boring. Too much product = infomercial.
3. **Specificity beats superlatives.** "Ten seconds" beats "super fast." "3pm crash" beats "more energy." Numbers and scenarios land; adjectives evaporate.
4. **The honest caveat is the trust anchor.** "I didn't think this would work." "I don't care if it's placebo." "I was literally about to return it." These lines INCREASE conversion by acknowledging skepticism.
5. **The CTA is casual, not urgent.** "Just try it." "Go get one." "Code's down there." Urgency in UGC reads as an ad. Calm reads as a recommendation.
6. **Low-density beats high-density in UGC.** Roe's cinematic reference runs 10+ effects per 3 seconds. UGC runs 2–4 effects per 3 seconds, maximum. Every extra effect is a vote against authenticity.
